# Quality Gates

Run the relevant checks before finalizing a learning output, radar report,
literature map, policy synthesis, or research design.

## Concept Gate

- Define fire refuge, fire refugia, or other central terms before using them.
- State whether the answer concerns human safety, ecological persistence, or
  both.
- Mark jurisdiction, geography, ecosystem, and scale when they matter.
- Do not treat every unburned patch as ecological refugia without functional
  evidence.
- Do not treat evacuation shelters, defensible space, or shelter-in-place as
  interchangeable with fire refuge.

## Source Gate

- Are source dates visible for current or recent claims?
- Are primary or near-primary sources used where possible?
- Are official sources used for policy, safety, and active-fire information?
- Are code/data links distinguished from paper-only claims?
- Are inferred research opportunities labelled as inference?

## Evidence Gate

- Does each major claim have a source, method, dataset, case, or clearly marked
  rationale?
- Are limitations stated?
- Is uncertainty discussed for models, maps, and policy recommendations?
- Are geography and scale transfer limits clear?
- Are vulnerable groups, equity, access, or governance considered when the task
  concerns human refuge or evacuation?

## Learning Gate

- Is the explanation matched to the user's stage?
- Are technical terms preserved for later search?
- Does the answer include a next reading, practice task, or search query?
- Is the output simple enough for beginners when beginner mode is requested?

## Radar Gate

- Are candidates ranked with a consistent rubric?
- Does the report distinguish trend signal from evidence strength?
- Are recent items actually recent?
- Are papers, datasets, projects, and policy sources not mixed without labels?
- Does the report end with concrete next actions?

## Research Design Gate

- Is the research question feasible with stated data, time, methods, and scale?
- Are baselines or comparison cases included?
- Are metrics aligned with the claim?
- Is there a first experiment that can falsify or support the idea?
- Are safety, ethics, and operational overclaim risks visible?

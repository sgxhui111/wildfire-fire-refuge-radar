# Research Design

Use this file when building related work, research gaps, project ideas,
proposal directions, methods, datasets, metrics, or first experiments.

## Research-Idea Intake

Identify:

- topic lane: human refuge, ecological refugia, remote sensing/AI, evacuation,
  risk, smoke/health, recovery, or governance
- geography and ecosystem
- available data or field access
- target output: class project, thesis chapter, paper, proposal, or prototype
- method comfort: qualitative, GIS, remote sensing, statistics, ML, mixed
  methods, or policy analysis
- time and compute constraints

## Gap Types

Useful gaps usually fall into one of these:

- concept gap: terms are used inconsistently or evidence streams are conflated
- data gap: missing labels, weak ground truth, limited geography, or poor
  temporal coverage
- method gap: models fail under cross-region, cross-season, cross-sensor, or
  rare-event conditions
- evaluation gap: metrics do not reflect decision quality, uncertainty, equity,
  or ecological function
- integration gap: fire ecology, human safety, planning, and AI/GIS are not
  connected
- implementation gap: useful research is not translated into planning,
  warning, refuge siting, or community practice

## Related Work Structure

Group by problem family, not by chronology:

1. What problem family does this work address?
2. What data and scale are used?
3. What methods dominate?
4. What does the evidence show?
5. What limitation remains?
6. How does the user's project fit?

Use a table:

`Theme | Representative work | Data/scale | Method | Finding | Limitation | Gap for this project`

## Project Idea Template

For each idea, include:

- Idea name.
- Problem.
- Why it matters.
- Hypothesis or research question.
- Data.
- Method.
- Baselines or comparison cases.
- Metrics or evaluation criteria.
- Expected contribution.
- Risks and ethics.
- First feasible experiment.

## Method Families

Match method to question:

- Conceptual review: definitions, typology, evidence map, debate synthesis.
- Systematic or scoping review: reproducible search, inclusion criteria,
  coding, and evidence gap map.
- Remote sensing/GIS: burn severity, active fire, fuel, recovery, refugia
  mapping, WUI exposure, or evacuation network analysis.
- Statistical modeling: drivers, correlations, causal caution, uncertainty.
- Machine learning: prediction, segmentation, forecasting, domain shift,
  interpretability, and calibration.
- Mixed methods: maps plus interviews, policy analysis, community knowledge, or
  practitioner validation.
- Decision support: scenario comparison, risk communication, operational
  constraints, and user needs.

## Evaluation Menu

Choose metrics that match the task:

- Classification/segmentation: precision, recall, F1, IoU, AUROC, calibration.
- Spatial prediction: spatial cross-validation, transfer tests, uncertainty
  maps, hotspot overlap.
- Fire refugia: persistence, severity contrast, patch size, connectivity,
  species/habitat support, recovery trajectory.
- Evacuation/refuge: travel time, capacity, access equity, failure scenarios,
  sensitivity to road closures.
- Policy analysis: jurisdiction fit, feasibility, accountability, evidence
  basis, implementation barriers.

Avoid metrics that look strong but do not support the actual decision.

# Concept Guardrails

Use this file whenever the task involves fire refuge, fire refugia, evacuation,
shelter-in-place, WUI, defensible space, or wildfire safety terminology.

## Core Distinctions

| Term | Usual meaning | Do not confuse with |
|---|---|---|
| Fire refuge | Human safety place, facility, or last-resort area used in wildfire emergency planning | Ecological refugia, ordinary evacuation shelter |
| Fire refugia | Ecological area that remains unburned or burns at lower severity, supporting persistence and recolonization | Human shelter or emergency refuge |
| Fire micro-refugia | Fine-scale ecological refugia driven by local topography, moisture, fuels, or microclimate | Any small unburned patch without ecological function |
| Evacuation shelter | Destination after people leave a hazardous area | A structure designed to withstand direct fire-front exposure |
| Shelter-in-place | Emergency strategy under specific official guidance and building/context constraints | A general recommendation for wildfire safety |
| Defensible space | Vegetation and fuel management around structures to reduce ignition risk | A guaranteed refuge |
| WUI | Wildland-urban interface where built structures meet or mix with vegetation | A single fixed risk class |

## Human Fire Refuge Lens

Analyze human refuge with:

- jurisdiction and official terminology
- warning lead time and evacuation feasibility
- road network constraints and traffic congestion
- refuge capacity, accessibility, and vulnerable populations
- building or site survivability assumptions
- governance, liability, communication, and maintenance
- equity, disability access, language access, and trust

Use official local guidance for policy or safety claims. Do not tell a user that
a specific place is safe during an active fire.

## Ecological Fire Refugia Lens

Analyze ecological refugia with:

- fire severity, unburned patch pattern, and repeated-fire persistence
- topography, moisture, fuels, vegetation structure, and microclimate
- species, functional groups, habitat continuity, and recolonization pathways
- spatial scale: micro-site, stand, catchment, landscape, or regional
- detection method: field survey, satellite burn severity, UAV, lidar, or model
- whether the patch is merely unburned or demonstrably functions as refugia

Avoid treating all unburned patches as ecologically meaningful refugia without
evidence.

## Integrated Questions

For work that combines human refuge and ecological refugia, keep two evidence
streams visible:

1. Human safety and planning evidence.
2. Ecological persistence and resilience evidence.

Then explain the integration mechanism, such as landscape planning, green
infrastructure, fuel treatments, community resilience, or conservation
prioritization.

## Safety Boundary

If the user asks about an ongoing fire, evacuation decision, or whether a
specific place is safe, respond with:

- consult local emergency services and official fire authority alerts
- follow evacuation orders and local warnings
- avoid personalized safety recommendations beyond general source routing

Then, if appropriate, offer to help interpret official guidance at a high level.
